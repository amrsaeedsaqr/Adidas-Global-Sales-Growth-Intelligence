1. Executive Summary (The Analyst's View)
  This project analyzes a $2.3M Global Sales Dataset for Adidas. The focus is on identifying regional growth drivers and segment performance using advanced analytical visual tools. The dashboard is designed for executive-      level decision-making, focusing on high-level KPIs like YoY (Year-over-Year) growth and Quarter-to-Date (QTD) performance.

2. Strategic Data Insights
  Aggressive Growth: A 46.88% YoY Sales Growth suggests a highly successful expansion or marketing strategy compared to the previous year.
  Segment Profitability: Using the Decomposition Tree, we can see that the West Region ($725K) is the strongest performer, with the Consumer Segment being the primary engine of revenue ($362K) within that region.
  Geographic Concentration: Sales are heavily skewed toward Tier-1 cities. New York ($0.26M) and Los Angeles ($0.18M) alone drive nearly 20% of total revenue, suggesting that urban-centric marketing yields the highest ROI.
  Seasonal Volatility: The "Sales by Month" chart shows a massive Q4 surge (October–December). This indicates a strong holiday-driven business model that requires significant inventory build-up in Q3.

3. Technical Toolkit Used
  KPI Tracking: Calculated YoY% and Sales QTD using [DAX for Power BI / Advanced Excel Formulas]

  Advanced Visuals: Utilized Decomposition Trees for root-cause analysis and Trend Lines for seasonality forecasting.

  Data Cleaning: Processed a complex dataset with 50+ columns, including Ship Modes, Product Categories, and Regional hierarchies.
